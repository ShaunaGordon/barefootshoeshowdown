Comparison Chart

A Comparison Of Both Shoe Types

Category Xero Shoes Vibram FiveFingers Thoughts
Overall Pretty even, overall. It boils down to asking yourself – what are your needs?
Longevity Xero Shoes win this one in my experience. The 5,000 mile warranty helps.
Quality The simpler design of the Xero Shoes makes quality control much easier.
Price $35 or $100? Xero Shoes would have to have a third the longevity to not win this one. Vibrams do have the upper hand with shoes made to withstand cold, wet weather, but you still need a pair for both warm and cold weather.
Comfort They tie, though each have their own reasons.
Learning Curve Vibrams win this one, if for no other reason than any given shoe has only one way to wear them. The DIY nature of Xero Shoes means a new wearer has multiple things to learn.
Groundfeel As great as the Vibram soles are for groundfeel, the Xero Shoes FeelTrue soles give you "high definition" groundfeel.
